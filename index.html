Part A: Apps/E Books

Bandlab

  • Can be used to record and create music
  • Can also be used in web browser
  • You can make music with other people!
  • It's free!
  • Can be found on google, IOS app store, and google play store

Spotify

  • Can be used to listen to music, podcasts, and audiobooks
  • Can be used on any device
  • Create playlists and save playlists made from different people
  • It's free!
  • Can be found in web player, google app store, IOS app store, and Microsoft store

Making Music

  • This is an e book for 6-12th graders
  • Can be used on any device
  • Teaches the history of music and music artists, and also strategies on how to release and create your own music!
  • The E book cost around $44.95
  • Can be found on Amazon.com

Part A: Devices/Hardware

Auto interface

  • Used for connecting microphones and other instruments to a computer
  • Can connect multiple instruments and microphones in your computer
  • Can cost from $50 up to $2,500

DAW (Digital Audio Workstation)

  • Helps produce, edit, record, and mixing audio
  • Adding effects to your music
  • Some DAWS are free and some are subscriptions

Monome

  • Creates triggering sounds and patterns for music
  • Used for live performances
  • Cost from $350 to $1,000

Touché (by Expressive E)

  • A MIDI controller that lets you controls sound with pressure
  • Adds expression to your music
  • Cost $300

Sensel Morph

  • A controller that can change into different kinds of instruments
  • Also used in live performances
  • Cost $300
